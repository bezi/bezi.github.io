Robotics

  • CMU Google Lunar X-Prize Team

    During the Spring 2014 semester, I began taking 16-815 (Advanced Mobile Robot Development), putting my skills toward the CMU LunarX initiative. Our goal is to improve on the "Andy" rover built by previous semesters in order to create a moon-ready rover by the beginning of May 2014. I am on the Avionics team, which is in charge of on-board electronics hardware and software. My primary contribution is to the firmware subteam, where I've been writing C using AVR Libc to write the firmware for the custom boards designed by my colleagues. The project is being led by Dr. Red Whittaker of the CMU Robotics Institute.

  • CMU Biorobotics Lab

    Starting during the fall of 2013, I began working for Dr. Howie Choset and the rest of the team at CMU's Biorobotics Lab. There, I have worked with firmware, communications, module repair, and project management software for the lab. The Biorobotics lab's largest project is the ModSnake project, which concerns itself with building modular snake robots for search and rescue, among other purposes.

    This spring, I began working on the lab's newest endeavor, known for now as "CappoBot". The first goal of the project is to create a self-stabilizing car that uses a gyroscope and the moment of inertia of it's wheels to stabilize in the air. I learned to use Solidworks to design the layout of the first version of the robot. I also learned to use a 3D printer to print some components.

Hackathons

  • HackPrinceton 2013

    On November 8-10, 2013, I traveled to Princeton. My teammates, Sinan Cepel, Jake Zimmerman and Andre Jia, got their first taste of the hackathon experience, while I had never competed in a 48-hour event. We were really interested in the data APIs we were shown by the sponsors, including the Yahoo Query Language, or YQL. We created Globalizr in Django to take advantage of this information and display it in an new way using WebGL.

  • HackCMU 2013

    On September 14-15, 2013, I worked with Justin Gallagher and Kirn Hans to make CMUEats, a website to help keep track of CMU's eating options. It was my first hackathon, and I learned a lot! We built CMUEats in Django and Bootstrap, although Justin and I would later port it to Node.js.

Web Apps

  • Angstlist

    Angstlist is the development name for a tradepost I'm writing for my bestest pals in CFA, specifically the visual artists. The front-end is being written in Jade and Sass, while the backend has been built with Node.js and MongoDB. It's my project for Winter Break 2013 (among other things, which are under wraps for now). I'd also never used MongoDB, so it's a learning experience. The current name is a play on words on Craigslist, and I really hope I can keep it. Guess it depends on the artists' sense of humor!

  • CMUEats!

    CMUEats, developed with Kirn Hans and Justin Gallagher at HackCMU 2013, is a site that allows people to easily check what food places are open around CMU, and when. Originally written in Django, Justin and I chose to port it to Node.js over Winter Break 2013. As of now, the front end is powered by Jade and Bootstrap. As my first web app, I'm extremely pleased to get moderate traffic even several months after the hackathon. Justin and I are continuing to add to CMUEats! as we find necessary or useful.

  • Globalizr

    In the fall of 2013, I was in a four person team that built Globalizr at HackPrinceton 2013. We chose to work with Django to build a system to display global information we'd found in the YQL database. The entire system is written in Python.
